# Portfolio Website Vision Document

## Overview

A personal portfolio website that serves as a comprehensive digital presence showcasing user research expertise, professional contributions, and thought leadership across multiple platforms.

---

## Purpose & Goals

### Primary Purpose
Create a centralized hub that demonstrates professional expertise in user research while providing easy access to all published work, contributions, and professional information.

### Key Objectives
- **Showcase User Research Projects**: Present detailed case studies of research work with methodology, insights, and impact
- **Professional Identity**: Comprehensive about page serving as a dynamic resume and professional narrative
- **Content Aggregation**: Centralize all external contributions (Medium, Substack, GitHub) in one discoverable location
- **Thought Leadership**: Highlight published articles and curated guidebooks that demonstrate expertise
- **Career Documentation**: Track and display professional contributions and evolution over time

---

## Target Audience

### Primary Audiences
1. **Potential Employers/Recruiters**: Seeking evidence of user research skills and professional experience
2. **Industry Peers**: Looking for insights, methodologies, and collaboration opportunities
3. **Content Readers**: Following published articles and guidebooks across platforms
4. **Professional Network**: Colleagues, mentors, and connections interested in work updates

### User Needs
- Quick access to relevant project examples
- Understanding of research approach and philosophy
- Easy navigation to specific types of content
- Ability to contact and connect professionally
- Discovery of published articles and guidebooks

---

## Core Features & Structure

### 1. Home/Landing Page
**Purpose**: Strong first impression and showcase of latest content activity

**Layout Structure**:

**Header Section**:
- Brief professional introduction (2-3 sentences)
- Value proposition highlighting user research expertise
- Clear navigation to main sections

**Latest Content Timeline Section**:
**Purpose**: Showcase recent activity and content creation with a chronological feed

**Timeline Display Features**:
- Reverse chronological order (newest first)
- Mixed content types: publications (Medium/Substack) AND guidebooks
- Visual timeline with dates
- Scrollable or paginated feed (showing last 10-15 items)

**Content Card Structure per Item**:
- **Date Badge**: Publication or update date
- **Content Type Indicator**: Visual badge/icon (Article, Guidebook, Update)
- **Title**: Clickable headline
- **Brief Description**: 1-2 sentence excerpt or summary
- **Platform Badge**: Medium/Substack icon for articles
- **Read Time**: Estimated reading duration
- **Call-to-Action**: "Read Article" or "Explore Guidebook" button
- **Tags/Topics**: 2-3 relevant topic tags

**Timeline Styles**:
- Clean card-based layout with left-aligned date markers
- Subtle connecting line between items (optional)
- Alternating or consistent card alignment
- Hover effects for interactivity
- Responsive design for mobile (stacked vertical layout)

**Filtering Options** (Optional):
- Toggle between "All", "Publications", "Guidebooks"
- Filter by topic/theme
- Search functionality

**Additional Elements**:
- "View All Publications" link to content page
- "Explore All Guidebooks" link to guidebooks page
- RSS feed subscription option
- Social media links
- Contact/connection options in footer

### 2. Projects Page (User Research Portfolio)
**Purpose**: Showcase user research work with depth and context

**Content Structure per Project**:
- Project title and overview
- Research objectives and questions
- Methodology and approach
- Key findings and insights
- Impact and outcomes
- Visuals (if applicable): journey maps, personas, research artifacts
- Timeframe and role

**Categories/Filters**:
- Research type (foundational, evaluative, generative)
- Industry/domain
- Methods used
- Project timeline

**Presentation Approach**:
- Case study format with storytelling
- Balance between detail and readability
- Emphasis on problem-solving and impact
- Protected confidential information with anonymization where needed

### 3. About Page (Professional Profile)
**Purpose**: Comprehensive professional narrative and credentials

**Content Sections**:

**Professional Summary**
- Current role and expertise areas
- Career philosophy and approach to user research
- Unique value proposition

**Experience**
- Work history with key responsibilities
- Notable achievements and projects
- Skills progression and growth

**Skills & Expertise**
- Research methodologies
- Tools and technologies
- Domains of expertise
- Soft skills and strengths

**Education & Certifications**
- Academic background
- Professional certifications
- Continuous learning initiatives

**Contributions Hub**
- Medium articles and publications
- Substack newsletter content
- GitHub repositories and code contributions
- Speaking engagements
- Community involvement
- Collaborative projects

**Personal Touch**
- Professional interests
- What drives your work
- Optional: personal interests that inform professional perspective

### 4. Publications/Content Page
**Purpose**: Aggregate and showcase all written content

**Organization**:

**Platform Sections**:
- Medium Articles (with links, dates, topics)
- Substack Posts (with links, dates, themes)
- External Publications (guest posts, interviews)

**Content Display per Article**:
- Title and excerpt/summary
- Publication date
- Platform badge/icon
- Direct link to article
- Topic tags
- Read time estimate (if available)

**Features**:
- Search functionality
- Filter by topic/theme
- Sort by date, popularity, or platform
- Featured/highlighted articles section

### 5. Guidebooks Page
**Purpose**: Curated collections of published articles organized thematically

**Structure**:

**Guidebook Format**:
- Guidebook title and description
- Purpose and target audience
- Table of contents with article titles
- Direct links to each article in sequence
- Estimated total reading time
- Last updated date

**Example Guidebook Themes**:
- "Complete Guide to [Research Method]"
- "User Research for [Industry/Domain]"
- "Getting Started with [Tool/Approach]"
- "Research Operations Best Practices"

**Display Options**:
- Grid or card layout for browsing guidebooks
- Individual guidebook detail pages
- Progress tracking (optional: mark articles as read)
- Download/save options for offline reading

## Design Principles

### Visual Identity
- **Professional yet Approachable**: Balances credibility with personality
- **Clean and Minimal**: Content-focused with clear hierarchy
- **Accessible**: WCAG compliant, readable fonts, appropriate contrast
- **Consistent**: Cohesive design language across all pages

### User Experience
- **Intuitive Navigation**: Clear menu structure, breadcrumbs
- **Fast Loading**: Optimized images and performance
- **Mobile Responsive**: Seamless experience across devices
- **Scannable Content**: Headers, white space, logical flow

### Content Strategy
- **Quality over Quantity**: Carefully curated projects and content
- **Storytelling**: Connect work to impact and human outcomes
- **Regular Updates**: Fresh content shows active engagement
- **Authentic Voice**: Personal yet professional tone

## Content Management Strategy

### Initial Setup
1. Audit existing content across all platforms
2. Organize projects with documentation
3. Create project templates for consistency
4. Compile all external links
5. Write about page content
6. Design and organize first guidebooks

### Ongoing Maintenance
- **Monthly**: Add new articles, update guidebooks
- **Quarterly**: Review and refresh project descriptions
- **Bi-annually**: Update about page, review all links
- **As needed**: Add new projects, create new guidebooks

### Content Creation Workflow
1. Document projects as completed
2. Track published articles with metadata
3. Evaluate themes for guidebook potential
4. Schedule regular content audits
5. Monitor broken links and outdated information

## Potential Challenges & Solutions

### Challenge: Keeping Content Updated
**Solution**: Monthly calendar reminders, simplified update workflow, content management system

### Challenge: Balancing Detail vs. Brevity
**Solution**: Use progressive disclosure, summaries with "read more" options, clear content hierarchy

### Challenge: Protecting Confidential Work
**Solution**: Anonymize details, focus on process over specifics, create sanitized versions, seek permissions

### Challenge: Standing Out in Crowded Space
**Solution**: Unique guidebook feature, authentic voice, focus on storytelling and impact, consistent updates

### Challenge: Technical Maintenance
**Solution**: Choose maintainable platform, document processes, set realistic update schedules
